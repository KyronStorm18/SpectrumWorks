A case study by Cindy Chan, Fiona Lin & Jeffrey De Leon

What is Spectrum Works?

Spectrum Works' main purpose is to connect employers and recruiters with autistic job seekers in a job fair format. This format breaks the stigma associated with autistic individuals contributing to the Canadian workforce

THE PROBLEM

The desktop and mobile interfaces are not user-friendly.

The desktop and mobile versions of their website were not responsive causing design and layout issues. Recruiters left the site without registering because relevant event information was not easy to find or non-existent. Without any recruiters, there's no job fair!

THE SOLUTION

Let's make it more user-friendly!

Improvements to the layout of information need to be made for employers to better navigate the site. This will encourage employers to feel confident about registering for the event!

HEURISTIC EVALUATION

To critique something you got to experience it first!

The best way to get an idea of the issues was to play the site ourselves. So we used heuristic evaluation to better understand the flaws in the site's design. From our evaluation, we discovered three main pain points.

PAIN POINTS

Inconvenient

There is no quick way to get all the necessary information and steps to register are not explained.

French & English

French and English were combined on the same page and need to be separated.

Architecture

Site architecture and organization needs to be improved.

OK! We got all the results. Time to make some hypotheticals.

With the results, we further developed the initial persona with an empathy map. We also created a priority matrix to determine the most important design changes that should be implemented. In addition, we created a user's journey and a UX scenario with a specific goal.

UX SCENARIO & GOAL

Janice wants to recruit special needs job seekers!

She finds out about the Spectrum Works virtual job fair. Janice registers, in order to find the inoramtion she wants.

Inclusivity and diversity at the forefront.

She wants to meet inclusivity and diversity goals by attending a job fair with special needs job seekers.

OPPORTUNITES

Organization

The site needs to be organized with clear instructions. The look and feel should be simple and minimal. The scale (across Canada) of the event should be evident.

Date & Time

Date, time and key highlights need to be prominent. The value of attending should be clear.

Fundamentals

Technical requirements and associated registration fees needs to be clearly explained before registration.

FINAL THOUGHTS

Spectrum Works NEEDS to make their site more appealing.

The site lacks story telling and direction. It was a challenge to write compelling stories to empathize with the audience. In the future, Spectrum Works should consider a job posting board, adding career tools, and tips for their employers and job seekers. In addition, activating chatbots for upcoming events would be great, to answer any questions prior to events. Thanks for following our process!
